Fourier Analysis

Fourier analysis is one of the fundamental tools of modern mathematics. Its central idea is that many functions can be represented as a combination of simple sinusoidal waves. This concept forms the basis of signal processing, image compression, communications, quantum mechanics and many other areas of science and engineering.

The Fourier transform converts a function from the time domain into its frequency representation, revealing the frequencies that compose the original signal.

\[ \begin{equation} F(\omega)= \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(t)e^{-j\omega t}\,dt \end{equation} \]

The inverse Fourier transform reconstructs the original function from its frequency spectrum.

\[ \begin{equation} f(t)= \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} F(\omega)e^{j\omega t}\,d\omega \end{equation} \]

Applications

Signal Processing

Filtering, spectral estimation, modulation and digital communications all depend heavily on Fourier methods.

Physics

Wave propagation, optics, heat transfer and quantum mechanics are naturally expressed in terms of frequency-domain representations.
